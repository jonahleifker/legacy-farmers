Grain Marketing
Intelligence

The biggest obstacle to profitable grain marketing is fear of production failure. We eliminate that fear by aligning your marketing goals directly with your crop insurance revenue guarantees.

Eliminating the "Fear of Selling"

Most farmers fail to lock in high grain prices during seasonal spring rallies because they are afraid they won't grow the crop to fulfill their contracts. If a crop failure occurs, they could be forced to buy back contracts at a loss.

**The Coordinated Solution:** When your crop insurance is structured to guarantee 85% or 95% of your expected revenue (using RP, SCO, and ECO), you know your minimum dollar floor. This allows you to pre-sell up to your insured bushels with absolute confidence, knowing that if you experience a yield loss, your insurance indemnity covers the contract buyout.

Data-Driven Target Triggers

We build grain marketing strategies based on objective cash-flow targets and historical seasonal price trends:

  • Breakeven Models: We calculate your exact cost of production per acre (seed, chemicals, machinery, rent, and financing) to establish profitable target sales points.
  • Seasonality Analysis: Historical data shows grain prices frequently peak in late spring/early summer due to planting uncertainty. We help you lock in prices during these optimal windows.
  • Flexible Marketing Tools: We assist in organizing cash forward contracts, hedge-to-arrive (HTA) contracts, and option strategies to maximize basis gains.

Ongoing Advisory Support

Market conditions shift daily. Our ongoing advisory service ensures you stay on track:

  • Weekly crop market briefs and basis trend reports.
  • Actionable SMS alerts when futures hit target pricing triggers.
  • Direct integration with your crop insurance reporting records.

The Value Add

On average, farmers using a coordinated insurance and marketing program capture an extra $0.30 to $0.60 per bushel by selling during spring rallies rather than harvest lows.

For a farm yielding 200 bu/acre on 100 acres, capturing an extra $0.40/bu results in $8,000 in additional net profit.
